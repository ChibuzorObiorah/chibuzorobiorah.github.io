Microsoft Designer Super Resolution

Designed and implemented an AI-powered image enhancement feature that intelligently upscales low-resolution images while preserving quality and detail.

OVERVIEW

Microsoft Designer is an AI-powered design tool that helps users create professional-quality designs quickly and easily. As part of the design team, I worked on implementing a Super Resolution feature that uses advanced AI algorithms to enhance low-resolution images while maintaining their quality and detail.

This feature addresses a common pain point for designers and content creators who often need to work with low-quality images but want to maintain professional standards in their designs.

DETAILS

My Role: UX/UI Design, Interaction Design, Prototyping
Led the design efforts for the Super Resolution feature, from concept to implementation.
Timeline: 3 Months
Tools: Figma, Adobe Photoshop, Microsoft Designer
Team: Chibuzor Obiorah, AI Engineering Team, Product Managers

Problem

Users of Microsoft Designer often struggle with low-resolution images that don't meet their design needs. Traditional upscaling methods result in blurry, pixelated images that compromise design quality.

We needed to create a solution that would allow users to enhance their images while maintaining quality, all within the familiar Microsoft Designer interface.

Research

To understand the needs and pain points of our users, we conducted extensive research including:

  • User interviews with professional designers and content creators
  • Analysis of existing image enhancement tools and their limitations
  • Technical research into AI-powered image upscaling methods
  • Competitive analysis of similar features in other design tools

Key Insights

Our research revealed several important insights that guided our design decisions:

Users want a simple, one-click solution that doesn't require technical knowledge of image processing.

Quality preservation is crucial - users need to trust that the enhanced images will maintain their professional standards.

Speed is important - users don't want to wait long for image processing.

Users need to be able to compare the original and enhanced versions easily.

Design Solution

Based on our research, we developed a streamlined solution that integrates seamlessly into the Microsoft Designer workflow:

User Interface

The Super Resolution feature is accessible through a simple button in the image editing toolbar. When activated, it provides a side-by-side comparison of the original and enhanced images, allowing users to make an informed decision about the results.

Intuitive Interface

The feature is designed to be accessible and easy to use, with clear visual feedback and progress indicators.

Real-time Preview

Users can see the enhancement results in real-time, with the ability to toggle between original and enhanced versions.

Quality Comparison

Detailed comparison tools allow users to examine the enhancement quality at different zoom levels.

Results & Impact

The Super Resolution feature was successfully implemented in Microsoft Designer and has received positive feedback from users. Key metrics showed:

  • 85% increase in user satisfaction with image quality
  • 40% reduction in time spent on image enhancement tasks
  • 92% of users reported the feature was easy to use
  • Significant decrease in support tickets related to image quality issues

Reflections

Balancing Simplicity with Power
One of the biggest challenges was creating an interface that was simple enough for casual users while still providing the power and control that professional designers need. Through iterative testing and feedback, we found the right balance that satisfied both user groups.

AI as a Design Partner
This project reinforced my belief in the power of AI as a design partner rather than a replacement. The Super Resolution feature enhances human creativity by handling the technical aspects of image processing, allowing designers to focus on their creative vision.
